# Plumber Feature and Value Hierarchy

## Product Lens

LeadOS should be organized and sold as a plumbing business operating system.

The hierarchy should always be:

1. business outcome
2. operational capability
3. software feature

Never invert that order.

## Level 1: Highest-Value Business Outcomes

These are the outcomes owners care about most:

1. more collected cash from the same volume of work
2. fewer leaks between booking and payment
3. better technician utilization
4. more consistent estimates and margins
5. stronger repeat business and membership revenue
6. clearer visibility into profitable work

## Level 2: Operational Capabilities

These are the capabilities that produce the outcomes.

### 1. Call-to-cash system

Business value:

- tighter job flow from first contact to payment
- fewer dropped handoffs
- faster cash realization

Needed capabilities:

- lead/call intake
- booking
- dispatch
- estimate approval
- invoicing
- payment collection

### 2. Dispatch and schedule control

Business value:

- higher technician utilization
- fewer late arrivals and scheduling errors
- more jobs completed per day

Needed capabilities:

- technician schedule visibility
- routing logic
- job assignment
- capacity awareness
- emergency prioritization

### 3. Flat-rate pricing and estimating

Business value:

- margin protection
- quote consistency
- customer confidence

Needed capabilities:

- price book
- estimate templates
- approval workflow
- service-type consistency

### 4. Invoicing and payment collection

Business value:

- less billing lag
- higher collection speed
- fewer unpaid jobs

Needed capabilities:

- immediate invoice generation
- payment request
- on-site collection support
- digital payment capture

### 5. CRM and customer history

Business value:

- better service continuity
- easier upsells
- stronger retention

Needed capabilities:

- address history
- prior work notes
- equipment records
- warranties
- unpaid balance visibility

### 6. Profitability reporting

Business value:

- smarter owner decisions
- better marketing spend
- better technician coaching

Needed capabilities:

- profit by job type
- profit by tech
- profit by ZIP
- revenue by source
- close/collection visibility

### 7. Membership and repeat-service system

Business value:

- steadier cash flow
- stronger lifetime value
- reduced acquisition pressure

Needed capabilities:

- maintenance plan tracking
- reminders
- renewals
- reactivation
- warranty follow-up

### 8. Field documentation

Business value:

- fewer disputes
- better training
- better callbacks handling

Needed capabilities:

- before/after photos
- notes
- approvals
- parts used
- job summaries

### 9. Marketing attribution

Business value:

- less blind spending
- better channel decisions
- more confident growth investment

Needed capabilities:

- source tracking
- call source tagging
- ZIP/source revenue mapping
- repeat vs new customer distinction

### 10. Standard operating workflows

Business value:

- more consistent staff performance
- lower dependence on memory and heroics
- better scalability

Needed capabilities:

- call handling workflows
- estimate presentation flow
- technician documentation standards
- invoice close-out process
- callback handling
- follow-up automations

## Level 3: Product Surfaces

These are the key product surfaces that should express the hierarchy.

### Public provider-facing sales surface

Purpose:

- sell the operating system in business language

### Dispatch desk

Purpose:

- run urgent and same-day work cleanly

### Price and estimate layer

Purpose:

- keep quoting standardized and margin-protective

### Job completion and payment layer

Purpose:

- shorten the time from completed work to collected cash

### CRM and customer record layer

Purpose:

- make repeat service and better care easier

### Reporting and profitability layer

Purpose:

- show where money is made or lost

### Retention and membership layer

Purpose:

- turn one-time jobs into recurring relationships

## Recommended Dashboard Hierarchy

If the product evolves further, the dashboard should emphasize:

1. Jobs in motion
2. Dispatch risk
3. Estimates awaiting action
4. Invoices unpaid
5. Payments collected today
6. Repeat-service and membership health
7. Profitability by tech, job type, and ZIP
8. Lead source to cash reporting

## Product Positioning Rule

Every feature should be explainable with this pattern:

`Feature -> operational capability -> money/time/profit result`

Example:

- flat-rate quote templates
- more consistent estimating
- stronger margin protection and higher trust at the kitchen table

## Final Priority Statement

The product should always make this sentence feel true:

`We eliminate admin friction and help plumbing businesses collect more money from the same volume of work.`
