# Provider Sales Page Architecture

## Goal

The provider sales page should convert serious plumbers and plumbing business owners by showing that the system helps them run more profitable work, not just generate more software overhead.

The page should sell:

- tighter operations
- better-fit jobs
- faster collection
- cleaner dispatch
- better visibility into profit

## Core Positioning

Primary message:

`This is not more software. It is a tighter operating system for profitable plumbing jobs.`

Supporting message:

`Book faster, route smarter, quote consistently, get paid sooner, and keep more value from the work you already win.`

## Section Order

### 1. Hero

Headline:

`Join the plumbing network built around profitable jobs`

Subheadline:

`LeadOS helps serious plumbing teams tighten call-to-cash operations across booking, dispatch, estimating, invoicing, payments, and follow-up.`

Hero chips:

- Call-to-cash
- Dispatch control
- Flat-rate quoting
- Faster collection

Primary CTA:

- `Apply to join`

Secondary CTA:

- `See customer-facing pages`

### 2. Pain Recognition

Section title:

`What owners are actually tired of`

Pain cards:

- too much admin between the phone call and the truck
- quotes that vary too much by tech
- invoicing delays after completed work
- weak follow-up and lost repeat business
- no clear visibility into which work actually makes money

Purpose:

- make the owner feel understood

### 3. Value Stack

Section title:

`Why owners switch`

Cards:

- Book faster
- Route smarter
- Quote consistently
- Collect sooner

Each card should describe a real operational improvement, not a feature label.

### 4. Operating System Modules

Section title:

`What the system should tighten first`

Modules:

- Call-to-cash flow
- Dispatch and schedule control
- Flat-rate pricing and estimating
- CRM and customer history
- Profitability visibility
- Membership and follow-up

Purpose:

- show that the system addresses the whole job lifecycle

### 5. Better-Fit Work

Section title:

`Better-fit jobs beat more random jobs`

Key ideas:

- preferred ZIPs
- issue fit
- response windows
- service-area alignment
- operator quality standards

Purpose:

- position the network as selective and quality-aware

### 6. Standards and Selectivity

Section title:

`Built for serious plumbing operators`

Criteria:

- clear service area
- reliable response behavior
- real capacity
- documented work quality
- consistent customer experience

Purpose:

- increase perceived opportunity quality
- repel low-fit applicants

### 7. Financial Outcome Framing

Section title:

`The value is in cleaner operations and more collected revenue`

Outcome blocks:

- fewer missed handoffs
- fewer dispatch mistakes
- faster invoicing
- faster payment
- higher repeat business
- clearer profit visibility

### 8. CTA Close

Headline:

`If you want a tighter shop, not just more admin, start here`

CTA:

- `Apply to join`

## Messaging Guardrails

Use:

- profitable jobs
- collected payment
- dispatch control
- flat-rate consistency
- repeat-service growth
- customer history

Avoid:

- “all-in-one solution”
- “AI-powered growth”
- “transform your business”
- generic lead-gen copy as the main story

## Conversion Principle

The provider sales page should convert because it makes the owner think:

`This could help me keep more money from the work I already do.`
